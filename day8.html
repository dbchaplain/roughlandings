Rough Landings Day 8: Vagueness of Vocation

“Go out and do something. It isn’t your room that’s a prison, it’s yourself.”
- Sylvia Plath

A place between two thoughts,
An unusual case of vagueness of vocation,
Another blinded soul upon the road.

Uncertainty;
An oddity among the many sure-footed peers,
I am uncertain of what purpose to pursue.

I was once told to go out,
Find something that I enjoyed
And if I could make money doing it
I had hit the jackpot.

Purpose;
What do you like, what do you enjoy,
And what brings you dismay that you must avoid?

I am getting the sense that it may not be up to me.
Too grand a scheme I am looking to find
In the little moments of the day to day
That make up a lifetime.

Look outside yourself
Towards someone else’s need,
And then you’ll start to see.

“The place God calls you to is the place where your deep gladness and the world’s deep hunger meet.”
Frederick Buechner
