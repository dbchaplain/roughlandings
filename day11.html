Rough Landings Day 11: Support Along the Journey

“Do not fear, I am with you; do not be dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you and help you; I will uphold you with my righteous hand.”
– Isaiah 41:10

“For I know the plans I have for you,” declares the Lord, “plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future.”
– Jeremiah 29:11

Coming to Embry-Riddle was one of the most exciting and terrifying experiences for me. I was thrilled to come to college, to meet new people, and to learn new things. It also scared me, I hadn’t been away from home for more than a week and now I was moving six-hundred miles away to a place where I didn’t even know anyone. I was embarking on the biggest adventure of my life and it was full of ups and downs. I learned a lot about myself during my first year at ERAU and I grew up, which wasn’t necessarily a bad thing.

My first semester at Riddle was rough, I was struggling with my courses and I was constantly homesick. Overcoming the loneliness and homesickness was hard, I came from a very close family that lived in the same town and it was a bit of a culture shock for me to come to this new place without any family nearby. As the semester went on I made new friends and that helped take my mind off being homesick. I was still struggling with one class and I was constantly studying but I could not figure it out. I had to learn to overcome failure, which was not easy for me to do. I felt like a disappointment because I failed a class. I didn’t know if what I was doing was a good fit for me or if I even needed to be here. I would call home and my parents would encourage me and give me great advice, but it made me miss home more. Looking back, I was fortunate to have parents that supported me the way they did.

Christmas break rolled around and I was so excited to go home. It was a much-needed break from school that allowed me to spend time with my family. They were all so supportive of me and what I was doing, I was excited to go back for my second semester but I wasn’t ready to leave home again. I can still remember driving back to Riddle after Christmas break and thinking to myself, “You could turn around now and be home before you would have made it back to Florida.” I prayed more during that drive than I ever have in my life. I prayed for the Lord to give me strength to overcome and conquer this new semester. I prayed that he would heal my heart and guide my steps throughout the year. It was during that drive that I realized I was in Florida for a reason and that God had not given up on me and I couldn’t give up on this.

I realized God had a plan for my life and he placed people around me to support and guide me during this journey. During my second semester, I found a church and they became my family away from home and they supported me when I needed it. I learned that sometimes you need to take a step back, take a deep breath, and trust the Lord. I was happier and the homesickness began to fade. I have made friends that will last a lifetime and I learned more about myself than I ever realized was possible. I couldn’t be more thankful for the opportunity I have been given to go to Embry-Riddle and follow my dreams. Without God and the support of my friends and family, I don’t think I would have ever made it this far.
