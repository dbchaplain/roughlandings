Rough Landings Day 3: Fading

Maybe you know what I’m on about
a poem about fading

I wish I could tell you
it was like fading into the sky
the way clouds do on a cold day
lightening into transparency
wispy bit by wispy bit
like a smudge removing itself from a
cerulean wall
turned sideways
I wish I could tell you
it was like the sun
on a really bright day
so bright the white
hot light
becomes the sky
and the sky becomes the sea in which you swim
and breathe
I wish I could tell you
there was still a silver lining
outlining
your form
when it was over
But that’s not how it is
at all
It’s more like a black hole
lots of black holes
sprinkled through your soul
eating up your space
the stars and the planets
the delicate, diaphanous nebulae
forming new yous and future yous
and eternal, beautiful potential yous
the yous that could be
being
gobbled up by
heavy
massive
moments
swallowing whole solar systems
and galaxies
vanishing
from the inside of you
out
as you
try to grip the sliding
dark matter
the gravity flux
the warping reality
bending and blurring
and contorting your edges
until you can’t see them
at all
until you’re nothing
but a figment
of your imagination
a “was” instead of an “is”
chasing after the racing
leaving
light
holding onto anything beautiful
hoping
it will anchor you
